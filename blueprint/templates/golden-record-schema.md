# Golden Record Schema (default)

The finished shape a single lead must carry. Editable per industry — this is the default
starter set. Every field is a property the refined lead must **prove about one person**.

| Field | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Full name | An actual named person |
| Title / Position | The role that would care about this purchase |
| Company name | The real company they sit in |
| What they buy | The product/category this lead is gold *for* |
| Has authority to buy | yes / no |
| Needs approval? | yes / no — and from whom |
| Phone number | for SMS |
| Email | for email |
| Buyer-intent signals | **why they are ready to buy *now*** — the live trigger (the spine) |
| Personalization payload | the human detail that makes the first message land (e.g. "noticed your son won his game yesterday"), possibly sourced from social |

**The spine is buyer-intent.** Real / in-role / authorized / reachable are table stakes; what
turns ore into a diamond is provable, right-now intent with a trigger behind it. More
personalization data = more value, because high-end buyers pay for high-intent leads carrying
enough data to personalize into a real message.

> Reachability and personalization *data-finding* are the refining stage's job (stage #3). This
> schema only declares that the finished lead must carry them.
