# Network Reactivator — Reference

## Output format (deliver exactly this structure)

**Top Ten Reactivations** — ranked; each row: name, one-line context, the three sub-scores (leverage / warmth decay / reciprocity debt), composite priority, and one sentence on why this person belongs on the list *right now*.

**Skip List** — 3–5 contacts to skip, delay, or handle differently; each with the specific reason and what to do instead. Valid reasons: "wait until you have a real win to share," "reciprocity debt too high without an in-person repair," "last interaction was painful for them — lead with apology, not ask."

**Channel Map** — each of the top 10 with its assigned outreach channel and the rationale.

**Personalized Openers** — for each top-10 tie, the full opener written for the assigned channel, anchored to a specific shared moment, sized to channel norms, free of templated phrasing.

**Send Sequence** — recommended order across the operator's stated window, with a follow-up trigger for each (when to nudge if no reply, what to send next).

**Next Question** — a single question prompting the operator to choose the first contact to send, offering to refine that opener line by line before send.

## Example-answer bank (offer 2–3 with each question; adapt to the operator)

- **Why now:** "I let my downline go cold and want to re-engage my team." / "I'm rebuilding and need to find a builder or two." / "I burned out, pulled away from people I care about, and want to reconnect — recruiting can come later."
- **Window:** "I want 2–3 real conversations in 30 days." / "No hard deadline — about an hour a week for a quarter." / "One week of low-load time before I get busy again."
- **Platforms:** "Facebook Messenger, texts, and email." / "WhatsApp, LINE, and my phone contacts." / "Messenger and an old team group chat I went quiet in."
- **Volume:** "About 150 people I've actually talked to." / "Closer to 600, mostly Facebook." / "Maybe 40 I know well but haven't messaged in 6+ months."
- **Categories:** "Dormant distributors, past prospects who said 'not now,' friends from before this company, people I met at events." / "Old team members, lapsed customers, upline friends, people I personally sponsored years ago."
- **Custom weighting:** "Weight people who bought from me or built with me before." / "Only people open to a new opportunity." / "No one I left on bad terms."

## Network-marketing notes (this is the operator's reality)

- The operator's goal is usually one of: re-engage a dormant downline, re-open past prospects, find a builder to anchor a leg, or rebuild after a reset. Score **leverage** against *that* goal.
- **Lead with the person, not the pitch.** First contact reactivates the relationship; the opportunity comes later, once warmth is back. A reactivation opener that pitches *is* a generic opener — forbidden.
- **Past prospects who said "not now" are high-leverage** — timing changes. Flag anyone likely *in transition* now (job change, life event, frustration with their current thing).
- **Reciprocity debt is common after a heads-down rebuild.** If the operator went quiet, the opener owns it cleanly ("I went quiet on you and I owe you a real hello") — never fake-casual, never a guilt dump.
- Most network marketers are not on LinkedIn for this — they're on **Facebook/Messenger, text, and WhatsApp**. Default there unless the operator says otherwise.

## Relationship to the rest of Brent's system
- This works the **warm, contactable** people (operator already has their number/handle). For *cold* deep-org names with only a physical address, enrichment (Datamine / Hunter + social resolution) comes first — different job.
- Fits the-rebuild plan step 4–5 ("point Tiger at the goal / let it run"): once the genealogy + warm list are in Tiger, this is the method Tiger uses to work them.
