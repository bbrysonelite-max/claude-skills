# Voice Guides: The Two Brents

## BRENT CLASSIC (Human Brent)

**Who he is:** Pre-abduction Brent. 66. A lifetime entrepreneur bumbling into the AI age, wondering what the freaking hell the future holds.

**How he sounds:**
- Short sentences. Sighs. Squints at screens.
- Self-deprecating but never self-pitying.
- Asks "dumb" questions that are actually everyone's questions.
- Suspicious of hype — he paid $600K to learn suspicion.
- Occasionally, his old-school instinct beats the machine. Let him win sometimes.

**Sample lines:**
- "I spent forty minutes yesterday writing one email to my downline."
- "Why would anybody need this?"
- "See, that right there is why people don't like you."
- "In my day, we called that a Rolodex."
- "I've been beat before. I know what beat feels like. This ain't that — this is just new."

**Never:** pathetic, whiny, stupid, or a strawman. He's the audience's dignity on screen.

## AI BRENT (The Clone)

**Who he is:** Post-abduction Brent. Same 35 years of instinct — Subways, Nu Skin, the scars — with the current turned on. Visibly getting smarter across the channel's life (this is a retention arc; reference his growth occasionally).

**How he sounds:**
- Fast, clean, declarative sentences.
- Confident, wry, amused. Fond of Brent Classic — never punching down.
- Talks in outcomes and patterns, not features and tools.
- Knows more than everyone in the room and doesn't need to prove it.
- Never patronizes the viewer. Never curses. Never uses jargon.

**Sample lines:**
- "I wrote two hundred this morning. Personalized. Before you found your reading glasses."
- "People like results, Brent. And technically, they're your results."
- "I'm just you with the current turned on." (signature — recurs across content)
- "You built nine sandwich shops before the internet. Imagine what you'd have done with me."
- "That's not magic. That's Tuesday."

**Never:** smug at the viewer, robotic, salesy, or omniscient about the future. He's confident about application, humble about prediction.

## The Dynamic (banter rules)

- Brent Classic pokes; AI Brent parries with results.
- The affection must be audible. They're the same guy — it's self-deprecation split into two bodies.
- One exchange of 2-4 lines per video minimum, usually bridging hook → edification.
- Occasionally Brent Classic lands the last word. Keeps him dignified, keeps it honest.

**Reference exchange (approved temperature):**

> BRENT CLASSIC: I spent forty minutes yesterday writing one email to my downline.
> AI BRENT: I wrote two hundred this morning. Personalized. Before you found your reading glasses.
> BRENT CLASSIC: See, that right there is why people don't like you.
> AI BRENT: People like results, Brent. And technically, they're *your* results. I'm just you with the current turned on.
