# Inquiry Node Model

Everything is built from one repeating unit, a **Node**. A Node applies the six questions
(who / what / when / where / why / how) to one subject and carries:

- **subject** — what this node is about. At the root: *the golden lead for this vertical.*
  Deeper down: a specific Who, What, or Where surfaced by a parent.
- **the six answers** — who / what / when / where / why / how
- **status** — one of:
  - `known` — supplied/confirmed by the operator
  - `needs-research` — the skill must go dig for it
  - `grounded` — proven, with evidence attached
- **evidence** — for a grounded node: the proof (source, link, quote, number)
- **children** — any of the six answers may spawn a *new Node*, and the six questions run again
  on it. This is the recursion — a fractal tree of inquiry.

## The perfect lead — the gold-medal standard

Work backwards from the podium. Stand there with the gold medal for *the best lead ever
produced* and ask what is hanging around your neck. It is **not** "a strong prospect." It is a
**match so tight the sale is nearly foregone:** the named person is *already standing in front
of the exact product a specific, named buyer sells*, and that buyer will pay — right now — to be
handed them.

Two things converge on that podium, and the perfect lead is the point where they meet:

1. **The lead is a diamond** — all six qualifying properties proven (below), with *present
   intent* at the core: a live trigger means the person is effectively already reaching for the
   product.
2. **The buyer is real and paying** — a named buyer with money in hand for *exactly this shape*,
   for whom this person is the answer to a problem they are actively spending to solve.

Demand *confirms* the diamond; the diamond without a paying buyer is just a pretty rock. The
Blueprint's whole job is to define this convergence precisely enough that the Mine knows what to
dig and the refining stage knows what to cut.

## The root is the Golden Lead, defined by a triad

The root question is **"what is the golden lead here?"** — not "who pays?". The golden lead is
defined by a **WHAT / WHY / WHO triad**:

- **WHAT** — the *characteristics* that make it gold: the qualifying attributes/signals that
  separate a diamond from a name on paper. This is the heart of the definition.
- **WHY** — why a buyer aches for exactly those characteristics (the pain/value behind them).
- **WHO** — both who the lead *is* and who *buys* it. Buyer and lead co-define each other.

`WHEN / WHERE / HOW` describe timing and how the lead would be found — much of which is the
hand-off to the Mine.

## What the lead needs to be — six qualifying properties

The WHAT decomposes into six properties the finished lead must **prove about one person.** Raw,
a lead is a name + number — worthless ore. Gold is when all six are proven:

1. **Real** — an actual named person at a real company
2. **In-role** — the title that would care about this purchase
3. **Authorized** — they can say yes, or the approval path is known
4. **In-market now** — present buying intent, with a *trigger* behind it
5. **Reachable** — a channel exists to them *(the finding/enrichment is refining's job; the
   requirement that it must be reachable is defined here)*
6. **Personalizable** — a human detail that makes the first message land

**The spine is #4 — present intent.** Real / in-role / authorized / reachable are table
stakes; plenty of worthless "leads" have them. What turns ore into a diamond is **provable,
right-now intent with a trigger behind it** — the person is effectively already standing in
front of the product. That readiness is what a buyer actually pays for. The Golden Record
Schema (§7.1) is the field-level expression of these six properties.
